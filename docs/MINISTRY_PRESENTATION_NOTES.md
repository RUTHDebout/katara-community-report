# Ministry Presentation Notes

These notes help present KATARA to ministries, municipalities, civil protection agencies, NGOs, and donors.

## One-line pitch

KATARA is a climate resilience and civic intelligence platform that combines satellite data, community reports, and AI to help municipalities anticipate, document, and respond to floods.

## Problem

Floods affect communities, roads, homes, schools, health centers, and local economies. Many reports from affected areas arrive late, informally, or without structured data. Municipalities often lack real-time community-level information and geospatial evidence for action.

## Proposed solution

KATARA provides:

1. Community flood and damage reporting.
2. Geolocated reports on a map.
3. Satellite/rainfall risk layers.
4. Exportable data for decision-making.
5. SMS/WhatsApp alert workflows.
6. AI assistant for documentation and response planning.

## Why it matters

- Faster reporting.
- Better local coordination.
- Improved evidence for emergency response.
- Better planning for drainage, roads, and vulnerable zones.
- Community participation in climate resilience.
- Stronger data for donor and public policy decisions.

## Target users

- Municipalities.
- Civil protection agencies.
- Environment ministries.
- Urban planning services.
- NGOs.
- Community champions.
- Researchers.
- Donors and development partners.

## MVP demo flow

1. A citizen reports a flood or damage event.
2. The report appears on the KATARA map.
3. An admin verifies the report.
4. The system exports CSV/GeoJSON data.
5. Satellite/risk layers are added for context.
6. The team generates a situation summary.

## Suggested pilot

### Pilot geography

Maritime region, Togo.

### Pilot duration

3 months.

### Pilot activities

- Identify flood-prone communities.
- Train community champions.
- Collect reports during rainfall events.
- Overlay reports with satellite and rainfall data.
- Produce weekly risk and damage summaries.
- Present findings to local authorities.

## Key indicators

- Number of community reports collected.
- Number of verified reports.
- Average verification time.
- Number of mapped risk areas.
- Number of alerts sent.
- Number of municipal decisions supported by data.

## Partnership request

KATARA may request:

- Technical validation.
- Access to local flood history.
- Administrative boundary datasets.
- Pilot authorization.
- Community focal points.
- Integration with local emergency response workflows.

## Risk management

- Protect personal data.
- Verify reports before public use.
- Avoid panic in alert messaging.
- Use clear severity levels.
- Keep human review in critical decisions.

## Closing message

KATARA does not replace public institutions. It strengthens their capacity with community-level evidence, satellite intelligence, and faster data flows.
